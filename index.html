James Harrison

The Universal Blood Donor

Here is a brief overview of how James Harrison became the "Baby Saver":

  • Life: Dec 27th 1936 - February 17th 2025
  • 1954: At age 14, James underwent major surgery requiring lots of donated blood. This inspired him to begin donating blood.
  • Soon after he began donating blood, it was discovered his blood contained rare antibodies to the D Rh group antigen.

    When an unborn baby has tested positive for D Rh antigens and their mother is negative, this can result in serious sickness, disabilities, and stillbirth. Through his donations of plasma, he has been able to provide over 2 million babies the antigens required to overcome such outcomes.
